Black Box Investigation

By Ezra Stark

The Mystery Black Box was created sometime in the late 2000’s by Kevin Sinusas. It has been opened since but only by him. The black box has three main observable components including but not limited to the box itself, a funnel on top, and a spout at the bottom. As part of the Cyan Team in class 4b it is my job to understand the workings of the Mystery Black Box.

Claim

The Mystery Black Box contains a tipping bucket and overflow container.

Evidence

We used the large graduated cylinder to measure input water and poured back into the graduated cylinder to measure output. We first tested the original black box then went on to test the model. The model was meant to emulate the results as close as possible but that didn’t quite go as planned.

Reasoning, Part I

The tipping bucket is a large device that tips all of the water inside of it out when the water reaches a certain amount. The overflow container is a container that overflows the water above a certain point.

Reasoning, Part II

The tipping bucket explains more water coming out than being poured in (See row 2 of the black box data)The container that overflows at a certain amount explains less water coming out than going in. (See row 5 of the Original Black Box data)

Reasoning, Part III

If there was only a tipping bucket then the data would not have less coming out than coming in because that would mean water is somehow staying in the tipping bucket. If there was only an overflow container then at a certain point the amount of water going out would equal the amount of water going in.
